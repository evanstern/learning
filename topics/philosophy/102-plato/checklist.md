# Understanding Checklist: Philosophy / 102 — Plato

> Check items off only when understanding is demonstrated (in your own words / correct reasoning), not merely acknowledged.

## 1. The Problem
- [x] What problem does Plato inherit from Socrates? (elenchus tears down but never builds — aporia as a dead end; "What is X?" questions with no answers on file)
- [x] Why did this problem exist in the first place? (Socrates wrote nothing, died refuted-but-unrefuting; Heraclitean flux vs. Parmenidean permanence — how can unstable particulars ground stable knowledge?)
- [x] What are the different ways it could have been solved? (sophist relativism, Antisthenes/Cynic minimalism, Megarian logic-chopping — vs. Plato's move: posit a stable intelligible realm)

## 2. The Solution
- [x] How does the theory of Forms work? (particulars participate in eternal, unchanging Forms grasped by reason, not senses; the Form answers the "What is X?" question the elenchus kept asking)
- [x] Knowledge vs. opinion (epistēmē vs. doxa) — why knowledge requires an unchanging object
- [x] The tripartite soul (reason / spirit / appetite) — and how it revisits the akrasia debate from 101 (inner conflict = parts disagreeing; "no one errs willingly" repaired by splitting the agent)
- [x] Justice in the Republic: city–soul analogy, each part doing its own work; philosopher-kings and why
- [x] The allegory of the cave — as an image of education, the Forms, and the philosopher's return (Socrates's fate encoded in how the returning prisoner is received)
- [x] Edge cases and failure modes: the Third Man regress, participation left vague, Plato's own late self-criticism (Parmenides) — the theory attacked hardest by Plato himself
- [x] Why dialogues, not treatises? (Plato never speaks in his own voice; early/middle/late grouping; the literary Socrates vs. the historical one)

## 3. The Bigger Picture
- [x] Why does this matter? (first systematic philosophy: metaphysics, epistemology, ethics, politics as one connected system; the Academy as the West's first enduring school)
- [x] What does it enable? (Aristotle — trained in, then rebelling against, the Forms; Neoplatonism → Augustine; "footnotes to Plato")
- [x] How does it fit the series arc? (Socrates asked the questions; Plato built the first full answer; Aristotle will bring it back down to earth)
