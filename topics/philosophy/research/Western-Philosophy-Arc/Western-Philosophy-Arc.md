# Western-Philosophy-Arc

The arc of Western philosophy from its pre-Socratic context through Socrates, Plato, Aristotle,
the Hellenistic schools, and onward through the medieval and modern periods — plus how that
history is conventionally taught to beginners. This branch exists to ground and sequence a
chronological Socratic learning series (educate topic `philosophy`) that starts at Socrates.

## Scope

**In:** survey-level facts about each period and school of Western philosophy; the lineages
between them; sequencing conventions, canonical beginner texts, and free authoritative resources.
**Out:** deep dives on individual figures (each lesson gets its own lesson-scope research vault —
Socrates' lives at `topics/philosophy/101-socrates/research/`); non-Western traditions; verdicts
about which lesson plan to adopt (analysis phase, if wanted).

## What is known

- Philosophy before Socrates was natural philosophy (physis/arche) plus the sophists' paid
  rhetoric, on the stage of direct Athenian democracy — [[Before-Socrates]].
- Socrates turned inquiry to ethics; Plato systematized (Forms, Republic) and Aristotle
  countered with empiricism, logic, and virtue ethics — [[The-Classical-Triad]].
- After Alexander, philosophy became an art of living: Cynics, Stoics, Epicureans, Skeptics,
  then Neoplatonism as the bridge to Christian thought; several schools claimed Socratic
  descent — [[Hellenistic-Schools]].
- The post-ancient arc runs medieval (Augustine, Aquinas) → early modern (Descartes → Kant) →
  19th century (Hegel…Nietzsche) → 20th century (analytic/continental split) — [[After-Antiquity]].
- Beginner courses conventionally run chronologically, start Socrates with
  Euthyphro/Apology/Crito, and lean on SEP/IEP/Perseus/Gutenberg for free texts —
  [[Teaching-the-History]].
