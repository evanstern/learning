# Teaching the History

How the history of Western philosophy is conventionally taught to beginners — sequencing norms,
standard first readings, and reliable free resources. Descriptive only; the lesson-plan decision
built on this belongs to the educate project, not this vault.

## Sequencing conventions

Two standard structures: **chronological/historical** (by period and thinker — the norm for
"history of philosophy" courses; Russell's *History* is the classic example) and
**problems-based** (by question — e.g., MIT's Problems of Philosophy 24.00). Many modern intro
textbooks hybridize. Ancient-philosophy courses conventionally open with the Presocratics
(~585 BC), reach Socrates as the ethical pivot, then Plato → Aristotle → Hellenistic schools,
often one module per figure/school. See [[_grounding]] Part 5.

## Standard first readings for Socrates

Euthyphro, Apology, and Crito (often with the Phaedo) are the canonical first readings: they are
short, each centers one ethical question, and together they narrate the trial-and-death arc as a
self-contained story. The grouping has ancient precedent (Thrasyllus' first tetralogy, 1st c. AD)
and modern institutional use (MIT sequences Apology/Crito and Euthyphro in its opening sessions).
SEP treats them as a class: "Plato's Shorter Ethical Works." See [[_grounding]] Part 5.

## Reference works and free texts

- **SEP** — free, peer-reviewed, expert-maintained (the most authoritative free reference).
- **IEP** — free, invited-contributor model without SEP's formal peer review; covers gaps SEP doesn't.
- **Perseus Digital Library** — Greek texts + translations (Burnet's 1903 OUP critical edition for Plato).
- **Project Gutenberg** — free Jowett translations of Euthyphro, Apology, Crito, Phaedo.

## Popular on-ramps (descriptive)

- Russell, *A History of Western Philosophy* (1945/46) — witty and continuously in print; criticized for accuracy/fairness, never a standard academic textbook.
- Kenny, *A New History of Western Philosophy* (4 vols, OUP) — popular yet serious, chronological with thematic tracking.
- Gaarder, *Sophie's World* (1991/1995) — novel-format history, 40M+ copies.
- Adamson, *History of Philosophy Without Any Gaps* — podcast/book series, skips no minor figures, 25M+ downloads by 2019.
- Crash Course Philosophy (YouTube, 2016) — 46 episodes on an intro-college curriculum.
- MIT OpenCourseWare 24.200 / 24.01 — full free syllabi and study questions.
