# Understanding Checklist: Philosophy / 101 — Socrates

> Check items off only when understanding is demonstrated (in your own words / correct reasoning), not merely acknowledged.

## 1. The Problem
- [x] What problem was Socrates responding to? (the sophists, received wisdom, Athenian civic life)
- [x] Why did this problem exist in the first place? (pre-Socratic context; democracy, rhetoric, and the marketplace of ideas in 5th-century Athens)
- [x] What were the alternative answers of his day? (sophistry, tradition/piety, natural philosophy)

## 2. The Solution — the Socratic method
- [x] How does the elenchus (Socratic questioning) actually work?
- [x] Why "I know that I know nothing" — what is Socratic ignorance and why is it a starting point, not a conclusion?
- [x] Key commitments: virtue as knowledge, care of the soul, the unexamined life
- [x] Failure modes: the Socratic problem (he wrote nothing — how do we know what he thought?), and why Athens killed him (trial, charges, the Apology)

## 3. The Bigger Picture
- [x] Why does Socrates matter — what changed in philosophy because of him?
- [x] What did he enable? (Plato, the Socratic schools, the very idea of ethics as inquiry)
- [x] How does he fit into the broader arc this series will follow (Plato → Aristotle → Hellenistic schools → onward)?
